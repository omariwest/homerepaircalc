Plumbing Leak Detection Cost: Hidden Leaks, Wall Leaks, Cameras, and Testing

Plumbing leak detection cost depends on where the leak may be hidden, how obvious the symptoms are, whether special tools are needed, and whether the plumber is diagnosing a wall leak, ceiling stain, bathroom leak, kitchen leak, slab leak, or emergency water problem.

Quick answer: how much does plumbing leak detection cost?

Plumbing leak detection usually costs about $100 to $500 for common residential diagnosis when the plumber can narrow the source with fixture testing, moisture clues, meter checks, pressure testing, or a basic camera inspection. More complex hidden leaks, slab leak detection, multiple wet areas, hard-to- access pipes, or urgent diagnosis can cost about $300 to $1,000+ before the actual repair begins.

Leak detection is not the same as leak repair. Detection finds the source. Repair stops the leak. The final bill may include both, plus wall access, pipe repair, drying, cleanup, or finish repairs depending on where the water went.

For a simple planning rule, budget low when the leak source is obvious. Budget higher when water is showing in one place but the pipe, valve, drain, or fixture causing it may be somewhere else.

Plumbing leak detection cost summary

Leak detection is the process of finding where water is escaping before repair work starts. Sometimes the leak is obvious, such as water dripping from a visible pipe under a sink. Other times, the only clue is a water stain, musty smell, high water bill, soft wall, warm floor, ceiling mark, cabinet damage, or meter movement when no fixtures are running.

The cost depends on how much uncertainty the plumber has to remove. If the source is clear, diagnosis can be quick. If water is traveling through a wall, ceiling, slab, cabinet, or floor system, the plumber may need more testing before cutting anything open.

The purpose of leak detection is to avoid random demolition. A good diagnosis helps the homeowner understand whether the next step is a simple pipe repair, fixture repair, valve replacement, drain inspection, wall access opening, slab leak repair, or emergency water shutoff.

For most homeowners, the important question is not only “What does leak detection cost?” It is whether the detection fee is separate from the repair, whether the plumber can credit the diagnosis toward repair, and whether the quote clearly separates finding the leak from fixing the damage caused by the leak.

1. Plumbing leak detection cost by situation

Basic leak diagnosis cost

Basic leak diagnosis usually costs about $100 to $250. This applies when the leak is visible or the symptoms point clearly to one fixture, valve, drain, toilet, faucet, or pipe connection.

This may include a service call, visual inspection, fixture testing, checking shutoff valves, running water through the suspected fixture, and confirming whether the leak is active.

Hidden wall leak detection cost

Hidden wall leak detection often costs about $200 to $600+. The plumber may need to check moisture patterns, fixture use, pipe routes, pressure clues, and whether the water is coming from a supply line, drain line, shower valve, toilet connection, or nearby appliance.

This is the correct path when there is a wall stain, bubbling paint, soft drywall, musty smell, or unexplained dampness. If the source is behind the wall, compare the next step with leaking pipe behind wall repair cost.

Camera inspection cost

A plumbing camera inspection often costs about $125 to $500+. This is most useful when the suspected problem is inside a drain, sewer line, blocked pipe, cracked drain, root intrusion, or hard-to-see waste line.

A camera does not solve every water leak. It is strongest for drain and sewer diagnosis. If the problem is a clogged or slow drain, compare it with drain unclogging cost.

Bathroom plumbing leak detection cost

Bathroom leak detection often costs about $200 to $700+. A bathroom leak can come from a shower valve, tub drain, toilet wax ring, toilet supply line, vanity drain, faucet connection, grout line, or pipe inside the wall.

Bathroom leaks can be tricky because water may show below the bathroom, beside the tub, near a baseboard, or on the ceiling under the room. Use bathroom plumbing leak repair cost when the suspected source is inside or near a bathroom.

Kitchen leak detection cost

Kitchen leak detection usually costs about $150 to $600+. Common sources include sink supply lines, drain fittings, dishwasher hoses, refrigerator water lines, garbage disposal connections, shutoff valves, and cabinet-hidden leaks.

Kitchen leaks often become more expensive when the cabinet base, flooring, toe kick, backsplash, or adjacent wall is wet. Compare that situation with kitchen pipe leak repair cost.

Slab leak detection cost

Slab leak detection often costs about $300 to $1,000+, depending on the home, pipe route, symptoms, and tools needed. Slab leaks are harder because the pipe may be under concrete or below finished flooring.

Warm floor spots, unexplained water usage, low pressure, foundation moisture, or the sound of running water can point toward this type of issue. For that situation, use slab leak detection cost.

Emergency leak detection cost

Emergency leak detection can cost about $250 to $1,000+ before repair when the call is urgent, after-hours, spreading, or connected to active water damage.

The main goal is stopping the water quickly. If water is actively dripping, spreading, or damaging a ceiling or wall, compare the full situation with plumbing emergency repair cost.

2. What is included in leak detection?

Leak detection should narrow the source of water before repair work starts. The exact process depends on the symptoms. A simple fixture leak may only need testing and inspection. A hidden leak may need more tools and more time.

Detection step Why it matters Cost impact
Visual inspection Checks visible pipes, valves, fixtures, stains, and damage Lower
Fixture testing Runs water through suspected sinks, toilets, showers, or drains Low to moderate
Meter check Helps confirm if water is moving when fixtures are off Low to moderate
Moisture checks Helps map where water may be behind a wall or ceiling Moderate
Camera inspection Useful for drain, sewer, or hidden pipe path issues Moderate to high
Pressure testing Helps identify supply line or slab leak problems Moderate to high

Ask whether the detection fee is only for diagnosis or whether it will be credited toward the repair. Some plumbers separate those charges, and some apply part of the diagnosis fee if you approve the repair.

3. What is usually not included?

Leak detection usually does not mean the leak is already fixed. It also may not include wall repair, paint, cleanup, or replacement of damaged materials unless the quote clearly says so.

Possible extra item Why it may be separate Planning note
Pipe repair Detection finds the source; repair stops it Often a separate quote
Wall access opening Cutting into walls may be needed after diagnosis Ask before approval
Drying wet materials Water damage work may involve another trade Do not close damp areas
Wall, ceiling, or cabinet repair Plumbers may not handle finish repair Can raise total cost
Emergency repair After-hours labor and urgent response cost more Confirm rate first if possible

This is why the leak detection cost can look reasonable, but the full repair can still be expensive. The detection fee is only the first step when the leak is hidden or has caused damage.

4. Signs you may need leak detection

Leak detection is useful when there is enough evidence of a leak, but the source is not obvious. The goal is to avoid guessing and cutting open the wrong wall, ceiling, cabinet, or floor area.

  • water bill increases without a clear reason
  • water meter moves when fixtures are off
  • bubbling paint or soft drywall
  • musty smell near a wall, vanity, cabinet, or floor
  • ceiling stain under a bathroom, kitchen, or laundry area
  • warm floor spot or damp flooring
  • low water pressure with no clear fixture issue
  • sound of running water when nothing is on
  • recurring stains after previous patching or painting

If the symptom is above or below a bathroom, compare the next step with bathroom plumbing leak repair cost. If the symptom is near a sink, dishwasher, refrigerator line, or garbage disposal, use kitchen pipe leak repair cost.

5. DIY checks before paying for leak detection

Some safe checks can help you explain the problem better before calling a plumber. Do not open walls randomly or keep running water through a suspected leak just to test it.

Safe homeowner check What it may show Do not do this
Check the water meter Whether water may be moving while fixtures are off Ignore an active leak
Look under sinks and around toilets Obvious supply, drain, or wax ring clues Disassemble plumbing if unsure
Note when the stain appears Whether it follows shower, toilet, sink, or appliance use Keep using the fixture if damage grows
Take photos of stains or swelling Helps the plumber see change over time Paint over stains before diagnosis
Shut off the affected fixture if obvious Can reduce ongoing damage Force a stuck valve

DIY checks are for narrowing symptoms, not replacing professional diagnosis when water is hidden, spreading, or active.

6. What increases plumbing leak detection cost?

Leak detection costs more when the source is hidden, the symptoms are inconsistent, or several systems could be involved.

  • water appears far from the actual leak source
  • multiple wet areas or repeated stains
  • ceiling damage below an upstairs room
  • bathroom leaks involving shower, tub, toilet, or vanity
  • kitchen leaks involving dishwasher, sink, disposal, or refrigerator line
  • slab, underground, or foundation-adjacent leak symptoms
  • camera inspection or pressure testing needed
  • after-hours or urgent diagnosis
  • older plumbing with several possible failure points

The highest detection bills usually happen when the plumber has to find a leak before any accurate repair quote is possible.

7. What happens after the leak is found?

Once the source is confirmed, the next step depends on what is leaking. A simple valve, supply line, or drain fitting may be repaired quickly. A hidden wall pipe, slab leak, ceiling leak, or emergency burst pipe may become a larger repair.

8. How to lower the cost

The cleanest way to lower leak detection cost is to give the plumber useful clues before they arrive. That does not mean guessing. It means documenting symptoms clearly.

  • take photos of stains, bubbling paint, swelling, and wet areas
  • write down when the water appears or gets worse
  • note which fixtures were used before the leak showed
  • check whether the water meter moves when everything is off
  • clear access under sinks, around toilets, and near shutoff valves
  • avoid cutting random holes before diagnosis
  • ask if diagnosis is credited toward the repair

Do not lower the cost by delaying an active leak. Waiting can turn a small detection visit into a water-damage repair.

9. When to call a plumber

Call a plumber when water is active, hidden, spreading, or unclear. Also call when there is a ceiling stain below a bathroom or kitchen, a soft wall, a musty smell, unexplained meter movement, or a water bill jump without an obvious fixture problem.

Call sooner if the leak may be inside a wall, under a slab, above a finished ceiling, near electrical fixtures, or connected to an upstairs bathroom. In those situations, early detection can reduce unnecessary opening, repair confusion, and water damage.

Find the source before patching

Do not repair drywall, paint over stains, or close a wall until the leak source is confirmed. If the situation is active or spreading, use plumbing emergency repair cost before treating it like a normal appointment.

10. Cost range notes

The ranges on this page are planning ranges, not contractor quotes. They are calibrated against plumbing leak detection, camera inspection, hidden pipe leak, slab leak, and emergency plumbing references. Local labor rates, diagnosis time, tools used, leak location, urgency, and whether the detection fee is credited toward repair can move the final price higher or lower.

Plumbing leak detection FAQ

How much does plumbing leak detection cost?

Plumbing leak detection usually costs about $100 to $500 for common residential diagnosis. Hidden leaks, slab leaks, camera inspections, urgent calls, or multiple wet areas can cost about $300 to $1,000+ before repair.

Is leak detection the same as leak repair?

No. Leak detection finds the source. Leak repair fixes the source. The final cost may include both, plus wall access, drying, cleanup, or finish repair depending on the damage.

Does a plumber charge separately for leak detection?

Sometimes. Some plumbers charge a diagnosis fee and then quote the repair separately. Others may credit part of the detection fee if you approve the repair. Ask before the visit starts.

How do I know if I have a hidden plumbing leak?

Common signs include a water bill increase, moving water meter, musty smell, soft drywall, ceiling stains, bubbling paint, damp cabinets, warm floor spots, or the sound of water when fixtures are off.

Can I find a plumbing leak myself?

You can do safe checks such as looking under sinks, checking toilets, watching the water meter, and noting when stains appear. Hidden leaks, wall leaks, slab leaks, or spreading water should be diagnosed by a plumber.

When is a camera inspection needed?

A camera inspection is most useful for drain, sewer, or hidden pipe path problems. It is not always the right tool for supply line leaks inside a wall.

Is slab leak detection more expensive?

Usually yes. Slab leaks are harder to locate because the pipe may be under concrete or finished flooring. Diagnosis may require pressure testing, acoustic clues, and more time.

Should I repair the wall before finding the leak?

No. Do not patch drywall, paint over stains, or close an opening until the leak source is confirmed and the area is dry.

What should I tell the plumber before leak detection?

Tell them where the stain or wet area is, when it appears, which fixtures were used, whether the water meter moves, and whether the issue is spreading.

When is leak detection urgent?

Treat it as urgent if water is actively dripping, the stain is growing, the wall or ceiling is soft, the floor is wet, or you cannot shut off the affected water supply.
